Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital project

There are a few rules businesses follow when it comes to General Data Protection Regulation, these are as followed:


  • The right to be forgotten – if consumers are no longer customers, or if they
    withdraw their consent from a company to use their personal data, then they have
    the right to have their data deleted.

  • The right to access –this means that individuals have the right to request
    access to their personal data and to ask how their data is used by the company
    after it has been gathered. The company must provide a copy of the personal data
    free of charge and in electronic format if requested.

  • The right to object – this includes the right of individuals to stop the processing
    of their data for direct marketing. There are no exemptions to this rule, and any processing
    must stop as soon as the request is received. In addition, this right must be made clear
    to individuals at the very start of any communication.
